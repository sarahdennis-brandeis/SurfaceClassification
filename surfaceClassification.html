Surface Classification



Instructions

By the Classification Theorem for Surfaces, any compact connected surface is either a 2-sphere, a connected sum of tori, or a connected sum of projective planes. Any such surface can be represented as a polygon with edges identified in pairs. We can classify a surface from its polygon representation using cut and paste operations.
To classify a compact connected surface, enter a text representation of a polygon into the input field at the top. A text representation of a polygon is a string of alphabetic characters where each letter occurs exactly twice. Use lower case letters to denote edges with a clock-wise orientation, and capital letters to denote edges with a counter-clockwise orientation.

Here are some examples you can try:

  • aa
  • aA
  • abBa
  • abcbca
  • AbCbca
  • aBccebEa
  • BcAabC
  • aBbA
  • ABeBea

Press "generate" to draw the polygon for cut and paste operations or "classify" to jump straight to a classification. With a polygon drawn in the drawing window, you can ...

  • cut: select two non-adjacent vertices and press cut
  • paste: select two edges with matching labels and of opposite directions and press paste
  • flip: reverse the order and direction of edges in the polygon
  • note: click in the drawing window to annotate with text from the note text field
  • hint: have prompted the appropiate next cut/paste operation
  • undo & redo: A stack based memory of cut and paste operations
  • reset: clear the input field and drawing window
